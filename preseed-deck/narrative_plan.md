# DORA Research, Inc. — Pre-Seed Investment Deck Narrative Plan

## Audience

- Angels and seed VCs

## Objective

- Raise a `$1M` pre-seed SAFE at a `$10M` pre-money valuation.
- Position DORA as a **B2C, mobile-first prediction market product** for sports-led audiences, with AI Ops as the structural advantage underneath the consumer experience.

## Core Story

DORA is not a B2B tooling company selling into operators. It is a consumer iGaming product. The deck should sell a simple thesis: mainstream sports fans want a faster, lighter prediction experience than traditional sportsbook UX or trader-native prediction venues currently offer. DORA turns live sports attention into simple yes/no markets, then uses an AI-native operating model to keep support, CRM, moderation, and market operations lean as the product scales.

The investor story therefore moves in this order:

1. Consumer gap
2. Product experience
3. Sports-led wedge
4. Repeat behavior loop
5. AI Ops moat
6. Pilot market and rollout
7. Team and raise

## Messaging Guardrails

- Treat DORA as **consumer-first** in every major slide.
- Keep `AI Ops` as the hidden operating edge, not the headline category.
- Keep regulation/compliance present but secondary; trust, settlement, and control belong in the stack story, not as the entire deck.
- Treat the `50%+ OPEX reduction` as a design target / structural ambition, not a proven operating result.
- Avoid unsupported traction claims beyond `MVP nearing pilot` and `pilot in Southeast Asia`.
- Keep prediction-market language accurate: yes/no positions, pricing, settlement, live markets, wallet, and repeat engagement.

## Slide Arc

1. Cover: DORA as a consumer prediction market built for mobile sports fans.
2. Consumer gap: sportsbooks overwhelm mainstream users while trader-native prediction products intimidate them.
3. Product: a lighter discovery-to-settlement flow built around live yes/no markets.
4. Behavior fit: why prediction markets map well to consumer attention and repeat play.
5. Sports wedge: sports is the first habit loop because the attention arrives pre-loaded.
6. Consumer loop: watch, predict, track, settle, return.
7. AI Ops moat: agents compress support, lifecycle, moderation, and market operations.
8. Why now: online gambling growth, sports betting dominance, and better cost curves.
9. Southeast Asia pilot: why this market fits the first launch.
10. Expansion: Africa, LATAM, and Eastern Europe after the first playbook works.
11. Trust stack: real-time markets, wallet rails, deterministic settlement, and control surfaces.
12. Business model: transaction rails, repeat event cadence, and lean cost-to-serve.
13. Team: John Brackens and a team with iGaming and software delivery depth.
14. Raise: `$1M` SAFE and use of funds.
15. Closing vision: the next breakout consumer format in iGaming is simpler, faster, and more native to mobile.

## Visual System

- Overall look: **light editorial investor deck**, not a dark operator dashboard.
- Backgrounds: warm ivory, soft sand, pale mint, and quiet blue-gray.
- Typography: `Outfit` for headlines, clean sans for body, mono only for prices and metrics.
- Product moments: use dark prediction-market cards and phone shells inside the otherwise light deck to make the product feel specific.

- Composition: asymmetrical layouts, generous whitespace, bold headlines, and fewer but better visual objects.
